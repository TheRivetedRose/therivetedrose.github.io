About

I am a cybersecurity student working towards a role in Security Operations, with a focus on detection, investigation, and structured incident response. I am developing practical blue team skills through hands-on lab work, emphasizing log analysis, system hardening, and disciplined documentation. Prior to transitioning into computer science, I spent over a decade in operational security roles within the U.S. Air Force and later in law enforcement.

In those roles, I worked in environments where decisions had consequences and documentation had to be clear, accurate, and defensible. I conducted investigations, prepared detailed reports, maintained evidentiary standards, and operated in situations that demanded composure and critical thinking under pressure. That foundation translates directly to security operations: methodical analysis, attention to detail, and accountability in how findings are documented and communicated.

I am now applying that investigative rigor to my technical growth. Through coursework and independent lab development, I am strengthening my understanding of networking, logging, detection workflows, and defensive frameworks. My goal is to contribute in a SOC environment where structured analysis, technical skill, and clear reporting matter.
